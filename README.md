# CFU Energy Cost Analysis: Natural Gas vs. Renewables & Battery Storage

A comprehensive analysis of energy generation costs comparing natural gas to renewable energy with battery storage, prepared for the **City Government of Cedar Falls, Iowa**.

## Files

### cedar-falls-energy-analysis.html
Interactive web-based analysis with six tabbed sections and Chart.js visualizations:
- Historical LCOE comparison (2009–2025) from Lazard data
- IRENA global weighted average renewable costs
- Projected LCOE through 2040 (EIA/NREL scenarios)
- Battery pack cost decline (BloombergNEF)
- Grid-scale battery system cost projections
- Cedar Falls–specific storage cost estimates

Open this file in any web browser to explore the interactive charts.

### cedar-falls-energy-analysis.pptx
16-slide PowerPoint presentation covering the same analysis in a format suitable for city council presentations. Includes executive summary, historical trends, projections, battery storage deep dive, and Cedar Falls–specific recommendations.

### viking-energy-center-report.docx
Comprehensive Word document analyzing CFU's proposed $78 million Viking Energy Center natural gas plant. Covers cost breakdown and financing, rate impacts, CFU's stated rationale, gaps in public documentation, alternatives comparison, and questions for consideration. All data sourced from CFU's 2026 Budget Book and public records.

## Data Sources
- Lazard Levelized Cost of Energy Analysis (v3.0–v18.0)
- IRENA Renewable Power Generation Costs reports
- EIA Annual Energy Outlook 2025
- NREL Annual Technology Baseline 2024
- BloombergNEF battery price surveys
- Cedar Falls Utilities 2026 Annual Operating Budget and Capital Improvement Plan
- CFU public communications and news reporting

## Usage
All files are self-contained. The HTML file can be opened directly in a browser — no server required. The PPTX and DOCX files open in Microsoft Office or compatible applications.

## Context
This analysis was prepared in April 2026 to inform Cedar Falls city officials about energy cost trends as they evaluate CFU's proposed Viking Energy Center, a 36 MW natural gas reciprocating engine power plant.
